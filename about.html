The Omniverse chamber of commerce (TOCC), registered in England &Wales as limited by guarantee is an association and network of global renowned business people and entities designed to promote their business, innovation globally. The name "Omniverse" depicts the futuristic vision of connecting other planet's (Human colony) businessmen or entities with us (Earth) in the future.

Chamber's Principles

Dual-House Governance Model

Establishing a dual-house structure within the Chamber of Commerce, consisting of the House of Advisors and the House of Executives, is to create a balanced and effective governance model that drives growth, innovation, and excellence across the business community. The House of Advisors will serve as the strategic and advisory body, providing insights, high-level guidance, and recommendations on long-term vision and policy direction. The House of Executives, on the other hand, will focus on the operational implementation of these strategies, executing initiatives, managing daily functions, and ensuring that the Chamber's programs and services align with its mission. Together, these two houses will work in synergy to strengthen the Chamber's role, fostering a dynamic environment for sustainable business development and member engagement.

House of Advisors

The House of Advisors is to act as the higher committee within the dual-house structure of the Chamber of Commerce, providing strategic direction and high-level guidance. Comprised of one distinguished Chairman, three senior advisors, and 20 advisory members, the House of Advisors will leverage the expertise of its members to support the Chamber's mission. This body will play a critical role in shaping policies, fostering connections, and driving initiatives that contribute to sustainable growth and prosperity for the entire business community.

House of Executives

The House of Executives within the Chamber of Commerce is to oversee the operational and administrative functions that drive the Chamber's initiatives and support its strategic goals. Led by the President of the House, the House of Executives includes one Senior Vice President, 10 Vice Presidents, 50 departmental Directors, and an appointed Accountant or Accounting Firm. This structure is designed to ensure efficient execution of policies and programs developed in collaboration with the House of Advisors. Each member of the House of Executives plays a specialized role, contributing their expertise to deliver impactful services, support Chamber members, and manage resources responsibly. Through this well-coordinated team, the Chamber will strive for operational excellence, transparency, and meaningful engagement with the business community.

Lifelong Honorary Founder

Establishing a lifelong honorary position for the Founder within the Chamber of Commerce is to recognize and preserve their vision, leadership, and ongoing contribution to the Chamber's success. As a lifelong honorary figure, the Founder will hold the unique right and privilege to advise all Presidents of the House of Executives, offering guidance that aligns with the Chamber's foundational values and long-term vision. Additionally, the Founder will possess both voting power and veto power, ensuring they can actively shape significant decisions and maintain a balanced, mission-driven direction for the Chamber. This role is intended to uphold the Founder's legacy while fostering a governance structure that remains true to its roots as it evolves to meet new challenges and opportunities.

Vote and Veto Committee

There will be Vote and Veto Committee within the Chamber of Commerce to establish a high-level decision-making body that upholds the integrity, balance, and strategic direction of the Chamber. This committee, composed of the Founder, Chairman of the House of Advisors, President of the House of Executives, two members nominated by secret vote, two members of a designated voting committee, and four representatives from the House of Executives, will wield significant influence with the authority to vote on and veto major resolutions and policies. The Vote and Veto Committee's role is to ensure that key decisions reflect the Chamber's mission, values, and long- term objectives, providing a structured and balanced approach to governance that supports sustainable growth and responsible leadership.

Chamber's Objectives

Technology Hub Development

To position the Chamber as a central technology hub, providing members with access to the latest innovations, digital tools, and technical expertise. This objective promotes technological advancement, enhances member productivity, and fosters a forward-looking business community.

Fintech Collaboration

To support the growth of financial technology (fintech) within the Chamber by connecting members with fintech resources, partnerships, and educational opportunities. This objective aims to enhance financial services, streamline business operations, and enable digital transformation.

Crowdfunding Initiatives

To facilitate crowdfunding opportunities for Chamber members, providing them with an alternative funding source to support business projects and ventures. This service aims to foster community support for member initiatives and encourage collaborative investment in innovative ideas.

Investment Opportunities for Startup Businesses

To encourage innovation and entrepreneurship by issuing investments and funding opportunities for startup businesses. This objective aims to support early-stage companies, foster economic development, and cultivate a thriving startup ecosystem within the Chamber.

Membership Opportunities for Individuals and Entities

To offer membership opportunities to both individual business professionals and business entities, creating a diverse and dynamic Chamber community. This objective enhances networking, collaboration, and access to resources that support business growth and success.

Visa Reference Letters

To provide official visa reference letters for individual members around the world, supporting their involvement in international trade and business activities. These letters aim to facilitate seamless entry processes and strengthen members' capacity for global business engagements.

Pre-Shipment Inspection (PSI) and Shipment Confirmation from Various Countries

To offer Pre-Shipment Inspection (PSI) services and shipment confirmations from diverse international locations, ensuring quality control, regulatory compliance, and timely delivery of goods for members. These services help protect international members' investments, reduce risk, and maintain supply chain integrity.

Work Permit Reference

To assist members in acquiring work permits for international assignments, by providing reference support and documentation. This service aims to enable the seamless mobility of talent and skills across borders, aiding members' expansion into global markets.

Business Arbitration Support

To serve as a resource for business arbitration, providing members with professional guidance and support in resolving commercial disputes fairly and effectively. This objective supports a cooperative business environment, fostering trust and stability within the Chamber's community.

Assistance with Business Disputes

To assist members in addressing and resolving business disputes by providing impartial advice, mediation resources, and access to legal professionals. This service aims to minimize conflict, support fair outcomes, and maintain positive business relationships among members.
